The following is a list of endangered Chinese dialects in the Northeast region:

哈尔滨农村方言
Harbin Rural Dialect

所处位置：黑龙江哈尔滨周边农村
Location: Rural areas around Harbin, Heilongjiang

濒危程度和保护现状：濒危高，城市化冲击严重，保护措施少
Endangerment and Protection: Highly endangered, severe impact of urbanization, few protective measures

发音特征：保留东北官话老形式，音调平缓，卷舌音明显
Phonetic Features: Retains older form of Northeast Mandarin, gentle tones, pronounced retroflex

词汇特征：农业相关词汇丰富，如“地瓜”地方表达
Lexical Features: Rich in agricultural vocab, local term for “sweet potato”

吉林山地方言
Jilin Mountain Dialect

所处位置：吉林省山区
Location: Mountainous areas in Jilin Province

濒危程度和保护现状：濒危中，年轻人迁移，部分文献记录
Endangerment and Protection: Moderately endangered, youth migration, partly documented

发音特征：受当地民族语言影响，音调独特
Phonetic Features: Influenced by local ethnic languages, unique tonal patterns

词汇特征：山区生活词汇，如“山货”变体
Lexical Features: Mountain-related vocab, e.g., variant of “mountain goods”

辽宁沿海方言
Liaoning Coastal Dialect

所处位置：辽宁沿海渔村
Location: Fishing villages along Liaoning coast

濒危程度和保护现状：濒危高，现代化冲击，无官方保护
Endangerment and Protection: Highly endangered, modernization impact, no official protection

发音特征：航海词汇多，辅音发音独特
Phonetic Features: Rich in nautical terms, unique consonantal sounds

词汇特征：渔业相关词汇，如“渔汛”地方表达
Lexical Features: Fishing-related vocab, e.g., local term for “fishing season”

黑河方言
Heihe Dialect

所处位置：黑龙江黑河市
Location: Heihe City, Heilongjiang

濒危程度和保护现状：濒危高，年轻一代少用，民间记录少
Endangerment and Protection: Highly endangered, less used by younger generation, scant folk records

发音特征：官话特征明显，音调单一
Phonetic Features: Prominent Mandarin features, monotone intonation

词汇特征：边境贸易词汇，如“对岸”地方表达
Lexical Features: Border-trade vocab, e.g., local term for “other side”

长白山方言
Changbai Mountain Dialect

所处位置：吉林长白山地区
Location: Changbai Mountain area, Jilin

濒危程度和保护现状：严重濒危，无系统记录，普通话冲击大
Endangerment and Protection: Critically endangered, no systematic record, strong Mandarin influence

发音特征：受朝鲜语和满语影响，音系混杂
Phonetic Features: Influenced by Korean & Manchu, mixed phonetic system

词汇特征：山林狩猎词汇，如“人参”变体
Lexical Features: Forest-hunting vocab, e.g., variant of “ginseng”
